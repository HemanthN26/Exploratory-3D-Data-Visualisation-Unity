# User Study Summary

## Overview

A lightweight usability study was conducted to evaluate how effectively users could interact with and understand the hierarchical 3D visualization system.

The evaluation focused on:

* interaction discoverability
* hierarchy understanding
* spatial similarity perception
* navigation usability
* exploratory behaviour

---

# Participants

Six participants took part in the evaluation.

Participants had varying levels of familiarity with 3D environments and data visualization systems.

---

# Study Structure

The study consisted of two components:

1. observer evaluation
2. participant feedback questionnaire

---

# Exploration Scenario

Participants were introduced to the system using a simple exploration prompt:

> “This system is used to explore a large image collection organised in 3D space. Please try to explore and understand how the images are organised.”

Participants were not initially told how interaction worked, allowing discoverability to be evaluated naturally.

---

# Observer Evaluation

The observer evaluation focused on:

* how easily participants discovered interaction
* whether users understood progressive reveal behaviour
* understanding of hierarchy structure
* ability to identify visually related image groups
* ease of navigation between hierarchy levels
* confidence during exploration

---

# Participant Tasks

Participants completed several lightweight exploration tasks:

* finding image groups with similar colours
* identifying related image clusters
* locating target groups inside the hierarchy
* navigating between hierarchy levels

---

# Questionnaire Evaluation

At the end of the session, participants completed a feedback questionnaire using a 5-point Likert scale.

The questionnaire evaluated:

* ease of interaction
* hierarchy understanding
* perceived similarity grouping
* navigation usability
* overall ease of use

Open-ended feedback was also collected regarding:

* confusion points
* usability improvements
* positive interaction experiences

---

# Observations

The study showed that:

* most participants quickly understood that the system was interactive
* hierarchical exploration was generally understood
* nearby images were consistently perceived as visually related
* the progressive reveal interaction model was intuitive for most users

Participants responded positively to:

* visual grouping of related images
* hierarchical exploration
* layered interaction design
* spatial navigation

---

# Usability Issues

Some usability challenges were observed during:

* deeper hierarchy navigation
* backward traversal between hierarchy levels
* orientation recovery after extended exploration

These observations highlight opportunities for future interaction refinement.

---

# Conclusion

The study demonstrated that the visualization system successfully communicated:

* hierarchical organization
* image similarity relationships
* exploratory navigation structure

while also providing valuable insights for improving interaction clarity and usability in future iterations.
